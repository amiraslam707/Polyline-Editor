📖 Polyline Editor — Complete Guide
📐 The 2D Canvas — How Coordinates Work

The big white canvas is your drawing board. Every point you place has three coordinates. Axis arrows are shown at the bottom-left of the canvas:

X
Horizontal
Increases left → right.
Set by where you click.
Y
Vertical
Increases top → bottom.
Set by where you click.
Z
Depth (3D only)
Default = 0 (flat).
Set with the Y slider in 3D.
💡 Point colours on the canvas show their 3D depth: cyan = 0 (flat), lime = mid-depth, orange = deep
✏️ Drawing Polylines — Step by Step
1
Press B or click the Polyline tool in the ribbon. The status bar shows DRAWING.
2
Single-click anywhere on the canvas to place each point. A coloured dot appears at each vertex, and a line connects them automatically.
3
Double-click to finish the current polyline. Press B again to start a new one.
4
Your polyline is now visible in the 3D view instantly — open it with Ctrl+3 to see it in 3D space.
🔧 Editing Polylines
M Move Point
Click near any dot and drag it to a new position. The line updates live.
D Delete Point
Click near any dot to remove it. The surrounding segments reconnect.
I Insert Point
Click anywhere along a line segment to add a new point between two existing ones.
Ctrl+Z Undo
Undo any action. Up to 50 levels. Works across both 2D and 3D edits.
🎨 Other Drawing Tools
P Pencil — free-hand drawing
L Line — drag for a straight line
E Eraser — drag to erase
F Fill — click to flood-fill a region
T Text — click canvas, type, click Place
K Eyedropper — click a pixel to pick its colour
🧊 What is the 3D View?

The 3D view shows your polylines as real 3D curves you can orbit around. Every point has X (horizontal), Y (depth — the 3D dimension you edit), and Z (vertical). By default all points are flat (Y=0). You can push individual points forward or backward to give your polylines 3D shape.

💡 Quick demo: Draw a polyline → press Ctrl+3 → click 🎲 Randomize Y → your flat lines instantly become 3D curves!
🔄 Both panels are live. Edit a point in 2D and it moves in 3D. Edit in 3D and it moves in 2D. There is only one set of polylines — both views show the same data.
📐 The 3D Axis System
X
Red arrow
Horizontal position — same as X on the 2D canvas.
Y
Green arrow
Depth — this is what you edit. Use the slider or Begin mode to set it.
Z
Blue arrow
Vertical position — same as Y on the 2D canvas.
💡 The dashed lines on the floor and walls are axis shadows — they show exactly where each polyline falls on each plane, making depth easy to read.
🎥 Navigating the 3D View
🖱️ Drag background
Click and drag the dark area to orbit the camera freely in any direction.
🖱️ Scroll to zoom
Scroll wheel zooms in and out.
⟳ Camera button
Resets camera to the default 45° view. Your polylines are not changed.
◀▶ Resize panels
Drag the divider bar between 2D and 3D panels to make either one wider.
✏️ Editing Polylines Directly in 3D

The 3D panel has its own editing toolbar with three modes. Changes in 3D immediately appear in the 2D canvas too.

B
Begin — create a new polyline in 3D
Click Begin (B) or press B while the 3D panel is focused.
Click anywhere on the floor grid to place points at Y=0 depth.
After placing, select each point and use the Y slider to give it depth.
Double-click to finish the polyline.
D
Delete — remove a point
Click Delete (D) or press D while 3D is focused.
Click near any glowing point to remove it. The polyline reconnects automatically.
M
Move — drag a point to a new position
Click Move (M) or press M while 3D is focused.
Click and drag any point. It moves on the floor plane (X and Z). Use the Y slider to adjust its depth separately.
💡 Select a point (any mode): Click near any glowing dot in the 3D view. It turns yellow and the XYZ readout appears below. Then use the Y Depth slider to push it forward or backward in 3D space.
🔧 Y Depth Controls
🎚️ Y Depth slider
Drag the slider to set depth from −400 to +400.
↑↓ Nudge
Arrow keys nudge the selected point Y by ±1. Hold Shift for ±10.
−10 −1 +1 +10 buttons
Quick nudge buttons below the slider.
📏 Set all → Y
Enter a value to push all points in the selected polyline to the same depth.
